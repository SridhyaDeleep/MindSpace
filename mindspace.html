Stress management

Student-focused tips that actually help

⏰

Pomodoro 25/5

Study for 25 mins, break for 5. Your brain holds focus better in short sprints.

📵

Phone-free hours

Set 2 hours a day with no notifications. Your anxiety will thank you.

🚶

Walk it out

10 minutes of walking lowers cortisol and clears mental fog before exams.

💧

Hydrate first

Dehydration mimics anxiety. Drink water before deciding you're stressed.

🌙

Wind-down ritual

Dim lights 1 hour before bed. Sleep is when memory consolidates.

🗓️

Weekly review

Sunday: list 3 wins, 1 lesson, 1 priority. Beats endless to-do lists.

Professional support

When and how to seek counseling

Talking to a professional isn't weakness — it's strategy. Here's how to know it might be time.

Signs it's time

You might benefit from talking to someone if…

• Stress lasts more than 2 weeks
• Sleep, appetite, or grades are slipping
• You feel hopeless or numb
• You're avoiding people you love
• Coping involves substances or self-harm

How it works

What a first session looks like

Usually 45–60 minutes. You talk, they listen. No pressure to share everything. Together you'll set goals and decide how often to meet. Most colleges offer free sessions.

On-campus

Your college counseling cell

Most institutions have a free, confidential student wellness center. Ask your hostel warden or check your college portal under "Student Welfare".
